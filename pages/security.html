Security

Built for security-conscious teams.

Every design decision in Equate starts with security. Here's how we protect your infrastructure and your data.

Encryption in Transit

All communication between the Equate agent and our cloud backend uses TLS 1.3. OOB sessions are additionally wrapped in an authenticated encrypted tunnel. No plaintext traffic ever leaves the device.

Encryption at Rest

All data stored in our AWS infrastructure is encrypted using AES-256. DynamoDB tables, S3 buckets, and any future RDS/Aurora instances use AWS-managed keys with optional customer-managed KMS keys for Enterprise plans.

Identity & Access

Role-based access control (RBAC) with granular per-site and per-device permissions. SSO via SAML 2.0 and OIDC on Pro and Enterprise plans. All authentication events are logged and immutable.

Immutable Audit Logs

Every OOB session, authentication event, and administrative action is written to tamper-evident audit logs. Logs cannot be modified or deleted by users. Retention periods are enforced at the infrastructure level.

Zero Trust Architecture

The Equate agent never opens inbound ports. All connectivity is outbound-initiated from the agent to our AWS backend. There is no exposed attack surface at the site level — even a compromised local network cannot reach the agent externally.

Infrastructure Monitoring

Our AWS infrastructure is monitored 24/7 using CloudWatch, GuardDuty, and AWS Security Hub. Anomalous activity triggers automated alerts to our security team. We maintain a formal incident response plan.

Responsible disclosure

Found a security vulnerability? We take all reports seriously and commit to acknowledging reports within 24 hours. Please email security@equate.io with details. We will work with you to understand and remediate the issue before any public disclosure.

We do not take legal action against researchers who report vulnerabilities in good faith and comply with our disclosure policy.
